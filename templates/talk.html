Supermarket food waste

The Problem of Supermarket Food Waste

Supermarkets around the world generate massive amounts of food waste, with estimates suggesting that up to one-third of all food produced globally is wasted. This waste has significant economic, social, and environmental impacts, including increased greenhouse gas emissions, lost resources, and hunger.

Causes of Supermarket Food Waste

Supermarket food waste can be caused by a variety of factors, including overproduction, unsold or expired products, quality control, and supply chain inefficiencies. Food waste can also result from consumer behavior, such as purchasing more food than they need or throwing away edible food at home

Consequences of Supermarket Food Waste

The consequences of supermarket food waste are significant, affecting both the environment and society. Food waste generates greenhouse gas emissions and contributes to climate change. It also represents a loss of resources, including water, land, and labor. Additionally, food waste contributes to global hunger and food insecurity, with an estimated 690 million people worldwide suffering from hunger.
